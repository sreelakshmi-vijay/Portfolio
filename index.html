About

I'm a final-year Integrated M.Sc. Data Science student learning to build intelligent systems that work reliably in messy, real-world conditions. My research focuses on understanding how models can generalize across different domains, handle noisy data, and learn from limited resources.

Through my projects, I've explored several challenging problems: making sign language translation work across different languages despite visual ambiguities, predicting wildfire risk across geographically diverse regions, detecting network intrusions in environments the model has never seen, and modeling pharmacokinetic dynamics using neural architectures.

What connects these projects is a focus on robustness and generalization—whether that's through semantic re-ranking in NLP, multi-task learning frameworks, domain adaptation strategies, or continuous-time modeling with Neural ODEs and Transformers.

I'm especially interested in problems where standard approaches break down: cross-domain transfer, low-resource settings, distribution shift, and multi-modal reasoning. I enjoy building end-to-end systems and understanding both theoretical foundations and practical implementation challenges.

Research & Learning

NLP & Multimodal Learning · Nov 2025 – Mar 2026
Sign Language Translation with NLP-Based Disambiguation
Explored how to combine visual recognition with semantic understanding for cross-lingual sign language translation. The challenge was handling visually similar signs that mean different things—a problem pure vision models struggle with. I built a hybrid pipeline that uses ANN-based keypoint classification augmented with Sentence-BERT semantic re-ranking to resolve ambiguities.
What I Learned
  • Multimodal feature extraction: combining hand landmarks, pose, and facial keypoints using MediaPipe
  • Semantic similarity modeling with Sentence-BERT for NLP-driven disambiguation
  • Designing evaluation metrics for low-resource translation (BLEU, Fix:Break analysis)
  • Working with extremely limited datasets and understanding when augmentation helps vs. hurts
  • Encoder-decoder architectures (LSTM, GRU) for sequence-to-sequence translation
MediaPipe Sentence-BERT PyTorch LSTM/GRU Low-Resource NLP
Multi-Task Learning · Nov 2025 – Mar 2026
Cross-Regional Wildfire Risk Modeling
Investigated whether a single model could learn fire classification, burned-area estimation, and fire weather index prediction across climatically different regions (Algeria, Morocco, Portugal). The project taught me about the challenges of multi-task learning—especially negative transfer when tasks or regions conflict with each other.
What I Learned
  • Genetic algorithm-based feature selection with custom cross-regional fitness functions
  • Region-aware gating mechanisms to handle heterogeneous data distributions
  • Dynamic uncertainty-weighted loss to balance multiple objectives automatically
  • Understanding when multi-task learning helps and when it hurts (negative transfer)
  • Dataset imbalance handling with region-balanced SMOTE strategies
  • Recognizing that simpler models sometimes outperform complex architectures on heterogeneous data
Multi-Task Learning Genetic Algorithms PyTorch SMOTE Cross-Regional Modeling
Domain Adaptation · Ongoing Research
Cross-Domain Network Intrusion Detection
Studied whether intrusion detection models trained on traditional IoT networks can generalize to Software-Defined Networking (SDN) environments. Built a CNN-BiLSTM architecture with attention and compared it against classical models. The results revealed a fundamental insight: deep models can overfit to source-domain patterns, while simpler instance-based learners like KNN preserve domain-invariant structure better.
What I Learned
  • Feature harmonization across 6 heterogeneous network datasets (14M+ samples)
  • Designing dilated CNN + BiLSTM architectures with attention mechanisms
  • Stacked ensemble strategies to combine diverse model predictions
  • Understanding domain shift and why deep models can fail under distribution change
  • Meta-learning approaches: training models on model outputs for improved robustness
  • The value of classical baselines—sometimes simpler is better for generalization
CNN-BiLSTM Attention Mechanisms Ensemble Learning Domain Shift Analysis Large-Scale Data Processing
Neural ODEs & Transformers · Apr 2026
Learning Pharmacokinetic Dynamics with Neural Architectures
Compared Neural ODEs and Transformer architectures for modeling drug concentration dynamics over time. Generated synthetic patient populations using Monte Carlo simulation and multiple numerical solvers (RK45, LSODA, Radau) to understand how different architectures handle smooth vs. stiff vs. coupled dynamical systems.
What I Learned
  • Continuous-time sequence modeling with Neural ODEs
  • Numerical differential equation solvers and their trade-offs for different system dynamics
  • Monte Carlo simulation for generating realistic synthetic patient populations
  • Transformer architectures for temporal prediction and their convergence behavior
  • Understanding model sensitivity: when Neural ODEs struggle with non-linearity, Transformers maintain stability
  • Benchmarking against classical methods (linear interpolation, exponential curve fitting)
Neural ODEs Transformers PyTorch Numerical Methods Monte Carlo Simulation
